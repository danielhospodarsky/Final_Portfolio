Here are some project highlights from this past semester at Iowa!

My Digital Product Management class was an immersive experience that helped me gain practical skills in managing and building digital products. Throughout the course, I learned a wide range of topics such as HTML, Python testing, Scrum project management, Google Analytics, and other relevant technologies.

One of the key highlights of the course was building websites using HTML. I gained a deep understanding of HTML, and I can now confidently build responsive and visually appealing websites. Additionally, I learned how to run tests using Python and how to implement Scrum project management frameworks to keep my projects organized and efficient.

Towards the end of the course, I was tasked with creating my final portfolio website, which was a culmination of all the skills I had learned throughout the semester. I combined my knowledge of HTML, Python, and Scrum frameworks to build an impressive website that showcased my skills and accomplishments.

Overall, my Digital Product Management class was a valuable learning experience that has taught me many different skills. I am excited to apply my newfound knowledge to my new job at Uline post-graduation!


Business Analytics Capstone Project:

In this class I was given the opportunity to work for the Atlantic Bottling Company, the distributor of Coca-Cola products in the midwest. My team was tasked to look into factors including ads, ad types, flavors of Coke and different packaging to see how we can maximize trade spend and increase volume.

Our first task was to clean the data. We were given spreadsheets of ad data for the past 3 years and weekly sales data. We needed to ensure that all of the spreadsheets we were working with were consistent with eachother. We reformatted the weekly sales data to create one column for week, a column for sales and a column for units. Originally, there were columns for each week of the year.

Once all the data was cleaned, we created dummy variables for ad types, different packages, and months. This allowed us to run linear regression models to see which variables were most significant.

After interpreting our results, we were able to give Atlantic Bottling some reccommendations on which ads they could run more often, cannibalization between packages, how to gain more market share, and what months they should be targeting. I enjoyed working with Atlantic Bottling and have gained many useful skills that I can bring to my new job at Uline post-graduation!
